About the Project

Visit Me is more than a travel app — it's a platform designed to bridge curiosity with discovery. The idea began as part of my final project for the Full-Stack Web Development diploma at Code Institute, and evolved into a passion-driven solution to help people explore both famous destinations and hidden corners of the world.

We’re building a travel space where users can not only find beautiful countries, cities, and landmarks, but soon, contribute their own discoveries through the upcoming Hidden Gems section. Whether it’s a quiet spot in a park, a tucked-away cafe, or a breathtaking view off the beaten path — Visit Me will become a community where these gems can be shared, rated, and remembered.

About the Developer

I'm Omer Ahmed, a full-stack web developer based in Austria with a deep appreciation for design, storytelling, and meaningful user experience. Before entering web development, I spent time learning and experimenting with digital design, content creation, and community-building platforms. This background shaped how I approach building tools that don’t just function — they connect.

Throughout my journey at Code Institute, I embraced Django as my primary backend framework, combining it with PostgreSQL, Bootstrap, and JavaScript to create a responsive, scalable, and interactive product. Every piece of the app — from rating systems to wishlists — was handcrafted to reflect real-world usability.

I believe technology should empower people to connect more deeply with the world around them. That’s what I hope Visit Me will do.

Where It’s Going

The first phase of Visit Me focuses on curated listings. The next phase will empower users to shape the platform — through uploading their own hidden places, reviewing, commenting, and building a shared travel culture built on local love and global curiosity.

My goal is to eventually evolve Visit Me into a mobile-first app with real-time contributions, user profiles, and map-based discovery — a go-to community for curious explorers everywhere.
